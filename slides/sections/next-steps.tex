\begin{frame}{Since mid-April: what we converged on}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Built the transport around cryptographic identity: Ed25519 public keys as peer identity and signed packets as the basic trust object.
        \item Stabilized the dual-stack direction: BLE discovery and nearby direct messaging, with \mbox{UDP/UDX} explored for longer-range connectivity.
        \item Worked through NAT traversal: public address discovery, hole-punching, well-connected friends, and rendezvous-style coordination.
        \item Added delivery machinery: ACK/read receipts, fragmentation for large payloads, Redux-backed peer/transport/message/friendship state.
        \item Added Noise sessions as hop-level channel security, then identified why relaying needs a separate end-to-end sealed message envelope.
        \item Reframed the thesis around a BLE-only path: remove UDP, analyze friend-based relaying, and compare it with direct BLE and open stranger relay.
        \item Connected the research plan to opportunistic networking, Sybil/misbehavior defenses, and Shapiro--Keidar work on grassroots/blocklace systems.
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
